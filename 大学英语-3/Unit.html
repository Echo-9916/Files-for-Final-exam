Unit 2

  1. The story of the African island Pate is presented as evidence of a possible connection to Zheng He's voyages.

  2. Zheng He's fleet was significantly smaller in scale and crew size compared to Columbus's voyages.

  3. Zheng He led seven major expeditions to the Indian Ocean between 1405 and 1433.

  4. Zheng He's father and grandfather were also famous explorers who sailed to Africa.

  5. The treasure ships featured an innovative design with watertight compartments in their hulls.

  6. The primary goal of Zheng He's voyages was to conquer and colonize foreign lands.

  7. Zheng He and his crew engaged in trade and established diplomatic relations with ports in Arabia.

  8. Zheng He died in China after retiring from his life as an explorer.

  9. A major reason for the end of the great voyages was the Ming court's shift of focus and resources to the Great Wall.

  10. Zheng He's voyages played a key role in spreading Chinese technologies like gunpowder and the compass.

Answer Key

  1. True. The article uses the story from Pate, including the descendants and porcelain shards, as an "echo" of Zheng He's voyages.

  2. False. Zheng He's fleet and crew were much larger. His crew totaled over 27,000, while Columbus's largest crew was only about 1,500.

  3. True. The article explicitly states "Seven times, from 1405 to 1433, the Chinese fleet set off..." and that all seven were led by Zheng He.

  4. False. The article says his father and grandfather had been to "distant places" and he asked them about their journeys, but it does not state they were explorers who sailed to Africa.

  5. True. The text mentions that the ships "featured divided hulls with several watertight compartments," calling it an "engineering innovation."

  6. False. The primary goal was to "establish ties" and "negotiated trade pacts," not conquest. The voyages were for diplomacy and trade.

  7. True. The article states he traded with Arabian ports like modern-day Yemen and Saudi Arabia and impressed their rulers with diplomacy.

  8. False. Zheng He died of disease in 1433 toward the end of his seventh voyage, not after retiring in China.

  9. True. The end of the voyages is attributed to the court turning its focus to the Great Wall, which drew military investment away from the fleet.

  10. True. The final paragraphs state his voyages were "essential to the spread" of gunpowder and the compass.

Unit 3

  1. The author finds the entire process of traveling, from the taxi ride to the airport security, to be a wonderful and exciting experience.（P1）

  2. According to the author, all travel is compulsory and necessary. (P4)

  3. The author believes that the main purpose of travel is simply to have fun and escape from problems.(P5)

  4. Experiencing a different culture can make a person more open-minded and aware that things can have multiple meanings.(P6)

  5. In Bulgaria, nodding one's head means "yes", just like in most other cultures.(P6)

  6. The creative benefits of travel come from a mere change in geographical location.(P8)

  7. The author argues that the confusion and surprises of foreign travel can have a lasting positive impact on our creativity.(P8)

  8. The author claims that after a vacation, one usually feels completely rested and does not need another break.(P9)

  9. The author suggests that to be more creative, we should actively think about our unsolved problems while traveling.(P5)

  10. The fundamental reason we travel, according to the author, is that distance and difference are secret foundations for creativity.（P9）

Answer Key

  1. F (The author describes it as a "burdensome series of running and waiting.")

  2. F (The article states that "most travel is decidedly optional," with only about 30% of long trips being corporate and compulsory.)

  3. F (The author states, "I'm afraid not," in response to the idea that we travel just for fun, and suggests it's for effective thinking.)

  4. T ("...the experience of an exotic culture endows us with a valuable open-mindedness, making it easier to realize that even a trivial thing can have multiple meanings.")

  5. F ("In Bulgaria, however, this response pattern is reversed – that is, head shaking means 'yes' and head nodding means 'no.'")

  6. F ("...this mental flexibility doesn't come from mere distance... Instead, this renaissance of creativity appears to be a side effect of difference.")

  7. T ("The same facets of foreign travel that are so confusing... turn out to have a lasting impact, making us more creative...")

  8. F (The author states the opposite: "More often than not, I need a vacation after my vacation.")

  9. T ("...we should be conscious of those domestic issues we just can't solve.")

  10. T ("We travel because we need to, because distance and difference are the secret cornerstones of creativity.")

Unit 4

  1. According to the author, the key difference between labor and work depends on the individual's personal taste for the job.

  2. Play is considered an activity that is important and valued by society.

  3. A person's job title or income level is a clear indicator of whether they are a laborer or a worker.

  4. Workers typically desire more hours of leisure than laborers do.

  5. Laborers view their working hours as meaningful and productive parts of their day.

  6. Technological progress has increased the amount of leisure time for many people.

  7. The article suggests that having too much leisure time always leads to positive outcomes, like self-improvement.

  8. The medieval aristocracy is used as an example of a group that had ample leisure time but did not always use it wisely.

  9. For workers, leisure is primarily a way to rest in order to return to work efficiently.

  10. The central argument of the article is that everyone must choose to be either a laborer or a worker, and this choice determines their happiness and satisfaction.

Answer Key

  1. True. (Paragraph 3: "Whether a job is to be designated as labor or work depends, not on the job itself, but on the tastes of the individual who undertakes it.")

  2. False. (Paragraph 2: "When we play a game... it is a purely private pastime; society does not care when or whether we play.")

  3. False. (Paragraph 3: "The difference does not, for example, correlate with the difference between a manual and mental job or between jobs with low or high income...")

  4. False. (Paragraph 4: "Workers are therefore more likely to dedicate more time to working, taking too little leisure rather than too much." Conversely, laborers believe "the more hours they have free for play, the better.")

  5. False. (Paragraph 5: Laborers "feel that the time they spend on the daily grind is wasted and doesn’t contribute to their happiness.")

  6. True. (Paragraph 6: "Increasing productivity... has reduced the number of necessary laboring hours.")

  7. False. (Paragraph 6 discusses how too much leisure can lead to wasting time on "addictive and trivial pursuits." Paragraph 7 then argues it's not necessary to have a toxic view of leisure, as it can be used positively.)

  8. True. (Paragraph 6: "The medieval aristocrats had an abundance of leisure time but often wasted it in trivial pursuit of games and fashion.")

  9. True. (Paragraph 4: "To workers, leisure means simply the hours they need to relax and rest in order to work efficiently.")

  10. True. (This is the central thesis of the article, as summarized in the final paragraph and the title itself: "Will you be a laborer or a worker?")

Answer Key

  1. T. (Paragraph 1 states they were training for "building and operating China’s first space station.")

  2. F. (Paragraph 2 mentions "more than 1,500 pilots applied," and the number was reduced to 14 after demanding tests, indicating a highly competitive process.)

  3. T. (Paragraph 3 quotes Yang Liwei saying, "Make use of outer space peacefully and for the benefit of all humankind.")

  4. F. (Paragraph 6 states that the astronauts have only one year to complete about 60 subjects, each of which normally requires a four-year university course, implying it is more demanding, not less.)

  5. F. (Paragraph 7 specifies that the centrifuge training is to help them resist g-forces "during lift-off," not re-entry.)

  6. T. (Paragraph 8 describes the "low-frequency oscillation" that caused him "almost unbearable pain," which he endured to realize the nation's dream.)

  7. F. (Paragraph 5 explicitly mentions that the astronauts owe "an enormous debt to their families for their thorough understanding and continuous support.")

  8. T. (Paragraph 10 quotes Jing Haipeng saying the honor belongs to the astronauts and "tens of thousands of scientists, engineers, and workers.")

  9. T. (Paragraph 11 states it became a source of national pride and sparked patriotic sentiments in Chinese communities worldwide, giving overseas Chinese "more courage and strength.")

  10. T. (The final sentence of the article directly states, "There is no limit to the vast universe or to China’s space exploration endeavors.")
